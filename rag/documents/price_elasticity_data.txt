## PRICE ELASTICITY COEFFICIENTS BY CATEGORY

Price elasticity (ε) is defined as: % change in quantity demanded / % change in price
A negative elasticity means: price decrease → demand increase

### Meat & Poultry
- Chicken Breast:     ε = -2.1  (highly elastic — consumers very responsive to discounts)
- Beef Mince:         ε = -1.8  (elastic)
- Pork Sausages:      ε = -1.5  (moderately elastic)
- Lamb:               ε = -1.2  (inelastic — premium product, price-insensitive)
- Cumberland Sausage: ε = -1.6

### Fish & Seafood
- Salmon Fillet:      ε = -1.4  (somewhat inelastic — viewed as premium)
- Cod Fillet:         ε = -1.7  (elastic — staple protein)
- Sea Bass:           ε = -1.1  (inelastic — luxury item)
- Smoked Salmon:      ε = -1.3  (inelastic — treat/occasion purchase)
- Prawns:             ε = -1.9  (elastic — convenience meal driver)
- Trout:              ε = -1.5

### Dairy
- Whole Milk:         ε = -0.5  (highly inelastic — staple, limited substitutes)
- Cheddar:            ε = -0.8  (inelastic)
- Greek Yoghurt:      ε = -1.3  (moderately elastic)
- Brie/Soft Cheese:   ε = -0.9  (inelastic — treat purchase)
- Butter:             ε = -0.6  (inelastic — staple)

### Bakery
- Sourdough Loaf:     ε = -1.6  (elastic — artisan premium but discount-responsive)
- Croissants:         ε = -1.8  (elastic — impulse purchase)
- Baguette:           ε = -1.7

### Produce
- Strawberries:       ε = -2.2  (very elastic — seasonal treat, promotion-driven)
- Raspberries:        ε = -2.0  (very elastic)
- Blueberries:        ε = -1.9  (elastic)
- Avocados:           ε = -1.6  (moderately elastic)
- Spinach/Salads:     ε = -1.4

### Ready Meals
- Chicken Tikka:      ε = -1.5
- Lasagne:            ε = -1.4
- Caesar Salad:       ε = -1.7  (elastic — convenience lunch)
- Pasta Bake:         ε = -1.5

## WEATHER DEMAND MODIFIERS

Temperature effects on demand (compared to 15°C baseline):
- Heatwave (>25°C):   Salads +20%, Beverages +25%, BBQ meats +30%, Ice cream +40%
                      Hot ready meals -25%, Soups -30%
- Very Hot (>30°C):   All chilled produce +15%, Reduce heavy meats by stock 20%

- Heavy Rain (>15mm): Footfall -18 to -22%, fresh produce demand -15%

## DISCOUNT RESPONSE CURVES

### Expected demand uplift from discount (approximation):
  10% discount → +15% unit demand (using ε ≈ -1.5 average)
  15% discount → +22% unit demand
  20% discount → +30% unit demand
  25% discount → +38% unit demand
  30% discount → +45% unit demand
  40% discount → +60% unit demand
  50% discount → +75% unit demand (ceiling effect — cannot exceed available customers)

### Diminishing returns:
  Above 40% discount, additional uplift drops sharply — shoppers suspect quality issues
  Optimal discount for highest revenue: 20-25% in most categories
  Optimal discount for waste clearance: 30-35% (accept lower margin)

## PRICE ELASTICITY FORMULA FOR SIMULATIONS

new_demand = base_demand × (1 + |ε| × (discount_pct / 100))
units_sold  = min(new_demand × days_remaining, current_stock)
waste_units = current_stock - units_sold
gross_margin = (revenue_at_discount - total_cost) / revenue_at_discount × 100
waste_prevention_value = (baseline_waste_units - waste_units) × unit_cost
