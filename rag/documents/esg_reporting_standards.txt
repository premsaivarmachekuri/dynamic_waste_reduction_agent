## APPLICABLE FRAMEWORKS

### WRAP Courtauld Commitment 2030
- Target: 50% reduction in food waste per tonne of food handled by 2030 (vs 2007 baseline)
- Scope 3 reporting required for all signatories
- Annual self-reporting with third-party verification recommended
- UK supermarkets handling >50,000 tonnes/year must report publicly

### UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)
- SDG 12.3: Halve per capita global food waste at retail and consumer levels by 2030
- SDG 13: Climate action — food waste contributes 8-10% of global GHG emissions
- SDG 2: Zero Hunger — food rescue and donation contribute to this goal

### EU Taxonomy (relevant for UK post-Brexit supply chains)
- Circular economy transition qualifies for sustainable finance taxonomy
- Food waste prevention is a "substantial contribution" activity

## ESG METRICS — DEFINITIONS AND CALCULATIONS

### Food Saved (kg)
  food_saved_kg = units_saved × weight_per_unit_kg

### CO2 Equivalent Avoided
  co2_avoided_kg = food_saved_kg × emission_factor
  (Use 3.3 as category-weighted default; use category-specific if available)

### Meals Equivalent
  meals = food_saved_kg / 0.300
  (Based on average UK meal weight of 300g — Source: WRAP 2022)

### Waste Value Prevented (£)
  waste_value_prevented = units_saved × unit_cost
  (Note: this is the cost of goods, not retail price)

### Social Value
  social_value = waste_value_prevented × 2.2
  (Source: New Economics Foundation — true cost of food waste to society)

### Trees Equivalent
  trees_equivalent = co2_avoided_kg / 21
  (1 mature tree absorbs ~21 kg CO2/year — UK Forestry Commission)

## REPORTING PERIODS AND CADENCE

- Daily:   Waste events logged, decisions executed
- Weekly:  Store-level waste reduction summary
- Monthly: Network-level ESG dashboard, trend analysis
- Annual:  WRAP/Courtauld compliance report, Scope 3 calculation

## BASELINE TARGETS (per store per year)

Assume 5 stores, UK mid-size supermarket:
- Waste target: <0.5% of perishable turnover value
- CO2 reduction: >15 tonnes CO2e/year vs prior year
- Food rescue rate: >30% of items that would otherwise be wasted
- Discount clearance rate: >85% of marked-down items sold

## DONATION GUIDELINES

Food banks and community organisations:
- Must have food hygiene certification (Level 2+)
- Temperature-controlled collection required for chilled items
- Minimum 24h remaining shelf life for donations (food bank policy)
- Donation value can be claimed for Gift Aid (if registered charity partner)
- FareShare and OLIO are recommended national partners

ESG credit for donations:
- Donation prevented waste = full CO2 credit applies
- Tax benefit (UK): cost of donated food is deductible business expense
- Reputational value: community benefit scoring for ESG reports

## WASTE REDUCTION MATURITY MODEL

Level 1 — Reactive: manual yellow stickers, no data-driven decisions
Level 2 — Proactive: rule-based alerts, some historical data use
Level 3 — Predictive: demand forecasting, automated discount recommendations
Level 4 — Autonomous (THIS SYSTEM): AI multi-agent, RAG-powered, real-time optimization
Level 5 — Collaborative: cross-retailer data sharing, industry-level optimization

This engine operates at Level 4, with ambitions toward Level 5 via network intelligence.
