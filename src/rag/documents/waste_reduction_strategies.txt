TITLE: Waste Reduction Strategies for UK Supermarket Perishable Goods
SOURCE: Dynamic Waste Reduction Engine Knowledge Base v2.0
CATEGORY: Operational Strategy

## CRITICAL ITEMS (≤1 Day to Expiry)

### Meat & Poultry
- Apply 25-35% markdown immediately when reaching same-day expiry
- Combined discount + loyalty coupon outperforms discount alone by 18% on average
- Chicken breast responds best to 30% discount (elasticity: -2.1)
- Beef mince: minimum 25% for viable margin; 30% recommended at ≤1 day
- Do NOT transfer same-day expiry meat — transit time risk exceeds benefit
- Donation to food banks is preferred when margin < 20% after markdown
- Heatwave conditions: accelerate to maximum markdown within 4 hours of store opening

### Fish & Seafood
- Most perishable category: act at HIGH risk (2 days), not just CRITICAL
- Salmon and sea bass: 25% discount at day 2 is more effective than 35% at day 1
- Cold chain integrity is critical for transfers — maximum 90 minutes transit
- Smoked salmon has longer transfer window due to curing process
- Cod and trout respond well to meal-deal bundling (increases demand ~22%)

### Dairy
- Milk: consumers highly price-sensitive; 20% discount moves stock efficiently
- Soft cheese (brie, camembert): premium buyers less elastic; 15% sufficient
- Greek yoghurt and butter have 3-day comfort zone for promotions
- Avoid transferring milk within 48h of expiry — recipient store risk too high

### Bakery
- Sourdough and artisan loaves: 40% markdown at end-of-day drives full clearance
- Croissants: sell by noon — morning 20% discount beats end-of-day 40%
- Freeze-and-return program viable for sourdough loaves (3-day window)
- Donation to food banks is highest-impact ESG action for bakery at <6h to close

### Produce (Fruit & Vegetables)
- Berries: 1.5-day effective shelf life after CRITICAL threshold
- Avocados ripen unevenly — sort by ripeness and price accordingly (20%/30%/50%)
- Strawberries and raspberries respond to loyalty coupon (sweet spot: 12-18% off)
- Pre-cut produce: always discount, never transfer due to oxidation risk

### Ready Meals
- Chicken tikka masala and lasagne: highest demand at dinner time (4-7pm)
- Time-targeted promotions (4pm push notification) outperform all-day discounts by 31%
- Transfer viable if ≥36h remaining and distance ≤25km

## HIGH-RISK ITEMS (2 Days to Expiry)

Decision priority matrix:
1. If transfer net saving > discount saving AND ≥36h remaining → TRANSFER
2. If weather reduces local demand → prefer TRANSFER to higher-demand store
3. If margin ≥35% at 20% discount → DISCOUNT (protects logistics costs)
4. If loyalty base >200 customers within 5km → LOYALTY_COUPON
5. If none viable → escalate to DONATE

## NETWORK-LEVEL OPTIMIZATION

Cross-store transfers should be evaluated at network level daily:
- Morning review: identify HIGH and CRITICAL items across all stores
- Match surplus stores to deficit stores using 7-day demand forecast
- Use weather differentials to route — sunny stores see +15% fresh produce demand
- Minimum transfer quantity: 10 units (below this, logistics cost >net benefit)
- Maximum transfer distance: 50km for ambient, 25km for chilled, 10km for hot

## ESG IMPACT CALCULATION STANDARDS (WRAP Courtauld Commitment)

- 1 kg food waste prevented = 3.3 kg CO2 equivalent avoided
- Average UK meal = 300g food
- £1 of waste prevented = approximately £2.20 social value (New Economics Foundation)
- Target: 50% reduction in food waste per tonne of food handled by 2030 (WRAP)

## MARGIN PROTECTION RULES

- Minimum gross margin floor: 25% (non-negotiable)
- Exception: donated items can go below floor (ESG credit offsets)
- Yellow sticker policy: 25-35% markdown is industry standard
- Loyalty coupon additional cost must be factored: net benefit = waste_saved - coupon_cost
- Transfer net saving = units_transferred × unit_cost - logistics_cost (£0.18/unit standard)
