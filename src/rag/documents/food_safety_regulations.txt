TITLE: UK Food Safety Regulations for Supermarket Perishable Management
SOURCE: Dynamic Waste Reduction Engine Knowledge Base v2.0
CATEGORY: Compliance & Regulation

## APPLICABLE LEGISLATION

### Primary Legislation
- Food Safety Act 1990 (as amended)
- Food Safety and Hygiene (England) Regulations 2013
- The Food Information to Consumers Regulation (EU) 1169/2011 (retained in UK law)
- Temperature Control Regulations (England) 2006

### Guidance
- Food Standards Agency (FSA) Guidance on Date Marking
- BRC Global Standard for Food Safety (Issue 9)
- HACCP Principles for Retail Operations

## DATE MARKING — CRITICAL DISTINCTIONS

### Use By Date
- MANDATORY for high-risk perishables: meat, fish, dairy, ready meals
- It is ILLEGAL to sell food past its use-by date in the UK
- Products must be removed from sale at close of business on the use-by date
- AI system rule: flag CRITICAL status 48 hours before use-by date for high-risk categories

### Best Before Date
- Applies to less perishable items: dried goods, ambient, frozen
- It is LEGAL to sell food past best-before date if quality is acceptable
- Supermarkets may sell past best-before at reduced price
- Not relevant to primary perishable categories in this system

### Display Until / Sell By
- Stock management guide only — NOT a food safety date
- Typically 1-2 days before use-by date

## TEMPERATURE REQUIREMENTS FOR STORAGE AND TRANSFER

### Chilled Products (maximum temperatures)
- Red meat, poultry, game:   ≤5°C (ideally ≤4°C)
- Fish and seafood:          ≤4°C (ideally ≤2°C)
- Ready meals:               ≤5°C
- Soft cheese:               ≤5°C
- Milk and cream:            ≤8°C (ideally ≤5°C)
- Hard cheese:               ≤8°C

### Transfer Temperature Rules
- Chilled goods must not exceed 8°C during transport
- Transit time limits:
  - Fish/seafood: maximum 90 minutes unrefrigerated
  - Meat/poultry: maximum 2 hours in insulated container
  - Dairy: maximum 3 hours if pre-chilled
  - Ready meals: maximum 2 hours
- Cold chain documentation required for transfers >25km

### Cold Chain Interruption
- Any temperature exceedance must be recorded
- "Two-hour rule": if chilled product above 8°C for >2 hours, must be discarded
- AI system must NOT recommend transfers that exceed these time limits

## DISCOUNT AND MARKDOWN RULES

### Legal Requirements
- No legal restriction on selling discounted near-expiry food (subject to use-by)
- Yellow sticker regulations: none specific — standard labelling rules apply
- Original price must be genuine (minimum 28 days at original price, or use "was" price rule)

### Best Practice (BRC Standard)
- Discount items must be clearly labelled with new price and original price
- Discounted items segregated from full-price to avoid confusion
- Staff training required: must not mix discounted and full-price batches

## FOOD DONATION COMPLIANCE

### Legal Framework
- Donated food is exempt from VAT (Zero-rated supply)
- No criminal liability for donating food of appropriate quality (Good Samaritan provision)
- Donor must ensure food is fit for human consumption at point of donation

### Recipient Requirements
- Registered food business or approved food bank
- Must have food safety management plan
- Temperature-controlled collection for chilled items (≤5°C)
- Minimum 24h remaining shelf life at point of donation (most food bank policies)

### Documentation Requirements
- Record: item, quantity, use-by date, temperature at donation, recipient, timestamp
- Retain records 12 months minimum
- AI system logs all donation decisions in BigQuery with full audit trail

## LABELLING REQUIREMENTS FOR DISCOUNTED ITEMS

Mandatory information on discount label:
1. New price (prominent)
2. Original price (must say "was £X.XX")
3. Any allergy information (unchanged from original)
4. Name of food (unchanged)
5. Use-by date (MUST remain visible — never obscure)

IMPORTANT: Use-by date must always be visible on discounted products.
The AI decision engine must never recommend removing or obscuring date labels.

## HACCP PRINCIPLES FOR WASTE REDUCTION DECISIONS

Critical Control Points (CCPs) relevant to discount/transfer decisions:

CCP 1: Temperature during storage — verify before discount decision
  - If temperature has been compromised, do not discount — dispose of safely

CCP 2: Temperature during transfer — validate logistics capability
  - Must confirm refrigerated transport available before recommending transfer

CCP 3: Date verification before sale
  - System must re-verify use-by date at point of discount application

CCP 4: Allergen integrity
  - Discounted items must maintain full allergen labelling — no degraded labels

## AI SYSTEM SAFETY CONSTRAINTS

The waste reduction AI must enforce these hard constraints:

1. NEVER recommend selling food past its use-by date
2. NEVER recommend transfers without validating cold chain capability
3. NEVER recommend discounts that obscure date labels
4. NEVER recommend donations with <24h shelf life remaining
5. NEVER recommend actions on items with compromised temperature history
6. ALWAYS log the final status of donated/discarded items for audit compliance
