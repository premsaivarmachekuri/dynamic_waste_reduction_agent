## TRANSFER DECISION FRAMEWORK

### When to Transfer vs. Discount
Transfer is preferred when ALL of the following are true:
1. Time to expiry ≥ 36 hours (to allow transit + sale window at destination)
2. Transfer net saving > best available discount saving
3. Cold chain transport available (refrigerated van or insulated crates)
4. Destination store has demonstrated absorption capacity (demand > current stock)
5. Distance ≤ 50km for ambient, ≤ 25km for chilled goods

Discount is preferred when:
- Time to expiry < 36 hours (not enough time for transfer + sale)
- Transfer cost exceeds potential waste saving
- Local demand is strong (weather, events)
- Product is highly perishable (fresh fish, bakery day items)

### Transfer Viability Formula
net_saving = (units_transferred × unit_cost) - (units_transferred × logistics_cost_per_unit)
viable = net_saving > 0 AND gross_margin_at_destination > 25%
Standard logistics cost: £0.18 per unit (UK retail benchmark, including driver, fuel, van depreciation)

## LOGISTICS COST STRUCTURE

### Per-Unit Costs
- Driver labour:    £0.06/unit (based on average 2-hour route, £18/hr)
- Fuel/mileage:    £0.05/unit (at 30p/km, average 10 units/km throughput)
- Vehicle:         £0.04/unit (depreciation/lease)
- Packaging:       £0.02/unit (reusable crates amortised)
- Admin/handling:  £0.01/unit
- TOTAL STANDARD:  £0.18/unit

### Refrigerated Premium
- Add £0.05/unit for refrigerated transport requirement
- Applies to: raw meat, fish, dairy, ready meals

### Minimum Economic Transfer
- Below 8 units: not economical (fixed route cost too high)
- Below 5 units: never recommend — pick-up fee alone exceeds saving

## ABSORPTION CAPACITY CALCULATION

How to estimate how much stock a destination store can absorb:

headroom = (daily_demand_at_destination × 3) - current_stock_at_destination
where daily_demand = average daily sales for same or similar SKU

If SKU not stocked at destination:
  estimated_demand = average_daily_sales_of_category_at_destination × 0.7

Maximum absorption cap:
  max_units = min(headroom, 5 × daily_demand)  # don't overstock destination

## COLD CHAIN MANAGEMENT PROTOCOL

### Pre-Transfer Checks (AI system must validate)
1. Temperature at source store: within acceptable range?
2. Refrigerated vehicle available at time of transfer?
3. Estimated transit time within cold chain tolerance?
4. Destination store refrigeration capacity available?

### Transit Temperature Monitoring
- All transfers require temperature logging (IoT sensor or manual)
- Maximum exceedance: 15 minutes above threshold during loading/unloading
- If temperature breach detected: transfer must be aborted and stock assessed

### Documentation Required
- Transfer request ID (AI-generated)
- Source batch ID and expiry date
- Units and weight
- Temperature at departure
- Temperature on arrival
- Receiving manager signature (digital)

## TRANSFER APPROVAL WORKFLOW

1. AI agent identifies transfer opportunity
2. System creates transfer record (status: PENDING)
3. Source store manager auto-notified (push/email)
4. Logistics team schedules vehicle
5. Receiving store manager confirms capacity (status: ACCEPTED)
6. Transfer executed (status: IN_TRANSIT)
7. Delivery confirmed with temperature log (status: COMPLETED)

Auto-approval criteria (no manual intervention needed):
- Saving ≥ £20 AND distance < 25km AND time to expiry ≥ 48h
- Always require manager acknowledgment for items > £100 value

## NETWORK-LEVEL TRANSFER OPTIMIZATION

Daily optimization run (recommended: 6am):
1. Query all HIGH/CRITICAL items across network
2. Build surplus-deficit matrix (store × SKU)
3. Solve multi-store assignment problem to maximise total net saving
4. Generate transfer manifests for each store pair
5. Output: ranked transfer list sorted by net saving (highest first)

Monthly analysis:
- Review which store pairs have highest transfer frequency
- Optimize vehicle routing for common corridors
- Negotiate dedicated logistics contract if >10 transfers/month on corridor

## PARTNER LOGISTICS PROVIDERS

Recommended UK cold chain logistics partners:
- Palletline (national network, next-day pallet delivery, cold chain available)
- TPN (The Pallet Network)
- Own fleet for transfers < 50km
- Third-party for ad-hoc transfers > 50km

For food bank donations (different logistics):
- FareShare: national network, will collect from stores on scheduled routes
- OLIO: community-level, no minimum quantity, 24h notice
- Local food banks: direct delivery, store van, 2h advance notice
